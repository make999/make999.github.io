MicroSec

Simplifying Security
Internet is Secure, so why IoT shouldn’t be: Over the years, many security protocols and standards have been put in place to secure the Internet. But security for IoT is still in infancy. We at MicroSec are trying to secure IoT systems as any other enterprise grade Internet system. This includes platform as simple as 8-bit 8051 or complex as 32-bit ARM.

Tiny Systems Do Require a security

Don't be misled by the capability of small IoT and sensor network devices. Certain applications are very sensitive to data and system security. Also exploited IoT devices recently have been utilized to perform DDoS attacks on other networks.

Communication Security

We are securing tiny devices by providing them the capability to have tiny certificates and transport/application layer security. We have optimized the mechanism of signature verification and key exchange to allow fast and energy efficient cryptographic operations.

Physical Security

We also provide physical security to the device by providing libraries for uitilizing hardware security management capability in selected devices. This also includes securely storing the keys and doing firmware check.

Security as a Library

Our motto is simplifying security and we live by it. We intend to provide cryptographic APIs to developers in such a way that they don't have to worry about the complex internal details about the security, but at the same time we also provide the flexibility of advanced users to tinker with the library. We know some of us like tinkering :)
